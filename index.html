Before You Begin

Let's find the right path for your complaint

The RVCS Complaint Policy requires that all complaints be resolved as close to their source as possible. You must attempt direct resolution before escalating to higher levels. This guide will walk you through each required step.

Important: Employment-Related Complaints

Please read before proceeding

⚠️
The School Director holds primary responsibility for the oversight and management of all school staff. The Board of Trustees does not directly manage hiring, firing, or day-to-day employment matters (except for the Director). The Complaint Committee may refer employment issues back to the Director.

Step One: Direct Contact

The first required step in the complaint process

Policy requirement: You must first contact those directly involved in the issue. For student concerns, this means speaking with the head teacher. Communicate the complaint clearly and identify actions that could lead to resolution.

Have you already spoken directly with the person involved (e.g., the head teacher or the individual responsible for the issue)?

1

Action Required: Contact Directly

You must complete this step first

🛑
You cannot skip Step One. The policy requires direct communication before escalating. The Complaint Committee will not review a case unless Steps One and Two have been completed.

Your next action is to contact the person directly involved:

  • For student/classroom concerns — speak with the head teacher.
  • Communicate your complaint as clearly as possible.
  • Identify specific actions that would resolve the issue.
  • Work cooperatively toward a resolution.
  • Keep notes of all conversations and dates.
💡
Once you have completed Step One, return here to determine your next step.

Step One: Outcome

Was your complaint resolved through direct contact?

After speaking directly with the person involved, was your complaint resolved to your satisfaction?

2

Step Two: School Director

Escalating to the Director of RVCS

Policy requirement: If not resolved in Step One, you must speak with the Director of River Valley Charter School and work together to develop a resolution plan. This plan may require review and revision during the process.

Have you already spoken with the School Director about this complaint?

2

Action Required: Contact the Director

Complete Step Two before proceeding

🛑
You must speak with the School Director before contacting the Board. The Complaint Committee requires documented evidence that Steps One and Two were completed.

Your next action is to meet with the School Director:

  • Contact the Director to schedule a meeting.
  • Clearly explain the complaint and prior attempts at resolution.
  • Work together to develop a plan to resolve the issue.
  • Allow reasonable time for the plan to be put into effect.
  • Document all meetings, dates, and agreed actions.
💡
Return here once you have completed Step Two, or after giving the Director reasonable time to implement a solution.
2

Step Two: Outcome

Has the Director resolved your complaint?

Have you given the Director a reasonable amount of time to implement a solution, and is the complaint still unresolved?

3

Step Three: Notify the Board Chair

Filing a formal written complaint

Policy requirement: Notify the Chair of the Board of Trustees and file a formal written report. Your report must document efforts made to comply with Steps One and Two. All named parties will receive a copy.
⚠️
Your written report must clearly document your Step One and Step Two efforts — without this, the Complaint Committee may return your case to a lower level.

Your written complaint report should include:

  • A detailed description of the specific complaint.
  • Dates and summary of your Step One direct conversation (with whom, what was discussed, outcome).
  • Dates and summary of your Step Two meeting(s) with the Director (what was agreed, what happened).
  • Why you believe the complaint remains unresolved.
  • What resolution you are seeking.
📋
Submit your written report to the Chair of the Board of Trustees. Copies will be sent to all parties named in your complaint.
4

Step Four: Complaint Committee Review

The Board's Complaint Committee will review your case

The Complaint Committee (composed of Board Trustees, including at least one officer) will review your written report. They will provide an initial response within 10 days and aim to issue a final report within 30 days of receipt.

What to expect during Step Four:

  • The Committee may return your complaint to the Director level if they believe more can be done there.
  • If Steps One and Two are found to be exhausted, the Committee will schedule meetings with all parties.
  • Meetings aim to gather information, determine points of conflict, and identify solutions.
  • The Committee may schedule a joint mediation meeting with all parties, or issue its own recommendations.
  • A formal written response will be issued at the end of the process.
⏱️
Initial response: within 10 days  |  Final report: within ~30 days (each case may vary).
4

Returned to Earlier Step

The Committee needs more done at the Director level

↩️
The Complaint Committee has determined that more effort can reasonably be made at the staff or Director level. Your complaint has been returned for further resolution attempts.

Next steps:

  • Follow the Committee's specific guidance on what additional steps to take.
  • Re-engage with the Director and document all new efforts.
  • If still unresolved, you may re-submit your written report with updated documentation.
5

Step Five: Board Report & Final Decision

The final stage of the internal process

After issuing its final report, the Complaint Committee presents an executive summary to the full Board of Trustees. The Board may be asked to vote on any policy or action items arising from the Committee's conclusions.

Are you satisfied with the outcome of the Complaint Committee's process?

!

External Escalation: Commissioner of Education

Beyond the school's internal process

🏛️
If the Complaint Committee has not adequately addressed your complaint, you have the right to submit it in writing to the Commissioner of Education.

To escalate to the Commissioner:

  • Submit your complaint in writing to the Massachusetts Commissioner of Education.
  • Include all documentation from the internal process (Steps 1–5).
  • Reference M.G.L. c. 71 section 89 and 603 CMR 1.00 (the laws governing Massachusetts Charter Schools).
  • You may request a copy of these laws from River Valley Charter School.
📄
Contact River Valley Charter School to request a copy of the relevant Massachusetts Charter School laws upon which to base your submission.
🎉

Complaint Resolved

We're glad your complaint has been resolved. The RVCS Complaint Policy is designed to find solutions that work for the whole school community. No further action is needed.
